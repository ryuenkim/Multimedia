☀️ #1

☀ Sun

🔗 Watch on YouTube

Age: 4.6 billion years

The center of our solar system, the Sun contains 99.86% of the solar system's mass and provides all energy for life on Earth.

☿️ #2

☿ Mercury

🔗 Watch on YouTube

Revolutions: 4.15

Orbital Period: 88 Earth days

Distance from Sun: 57.9 million km

The smallest and innermost planet, Mercury has extreme temperature variations and a surface scarred by countless impact craters.

♀️ #3

♀ Venus

🔗 Watch on YouTube

Atmosphere: Carbon Dioxide

Temperature: 465°C (hottest planet)

The hottest planet in our solar system with a thick, toxic atmosphere and a surface completely covered in volcanic features.

♁️ #4

♁ Earth

🔗 Watch on YouTube

Moons: 1 (The Moon)

Orbital Period: 365.25 days

Distance from Sun: 149.6 million km (1 AU)

Our home planet, the only known world to harbor life with its perfect conditions for water and diverse ecosystems.

☾ #5

☾ Moon

🔗 Watch on YouTube

Diameter: 3,474 km

Orbital Period: 27.3 Earth days

Earth's natural satellite, responsible for tides and plays a crucial role in Earth's climate and life cycles.

♂️ #6

♂ Mars

🔗 Watch on YouTube

Color: Red (from iron oxide)

Orbital Period: 687 Earth days

Distance from Sun: 227.9 million km

The red planet, a primary target for human exploration with evidence of past water and potential for future colonies.

♃️ #7

♃ Jupiter

🔗 Watch on YouTube

Size: 11 times wider than Earth

Moons: 95+ known moons

Notable Feature: Great Red Spot (massive storm)

The largest planet, a gas giant with intense storms and a powerful magnetic field, serving as a protector of the inner planets.

♄️ #8

♄ Saturn

🔗 Watch on YouTube

Rings: Prominent ring system

Moons: 146+ known moons

Notable Moon: Titan (has methane lakes)

Known for its spectacular ring system, Saturn is a gas giant with diverse moons and fascinating atmospheric dynamics.

♅️ #9

♅ Uranus

🔗 Watch on YouTube

Rotation: Rotates on its side (98° tilt)

Moons: 27+ known moons

Color: Cyan/blue from methane atmosphere

An ice giant with a unique axial tilt, tilted 98° from vertical, making it the most tilted planet in our solar system.

♆️ #10

♆ Neptune

🔗 Watch on YouTube

Winds: Fastest in solar system (2,100 km/h)

Color: Deep blue from methane

Orbital Period: 165 Earth years

The windiest planet and farthest from the Sun, Neptune has intense storms and a dynamic atmosphere despite receiving minimal solar energy.

♇ #11

♇ Pluto

🔗 Watch on YouTube

Classification: Dwarf Planet

Diameter: 2,377 km

Orbital Period: 248 Earth years

Once considered the ninth planet, Pluto is now classified as a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt, with a heart-shaped region and five known moons.
